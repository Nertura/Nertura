# 01 — Nertura Experience Principles

## Purpose

Define the emotional contract between Nertura and the farmer across all surfaces.

---

## Principles

### 1. Calm over urgency

Agriculture decisions are high-stakes. UI must not create artificial panic. Critical alerts are visible; everything else is measured.

### 2. Guidance over information dump

Show the **next best action** first. Details expand on demand. Never lead with raw data tables on farmer surfaces.

### 3. Trust before conversion

Guest users must experience useful intelligence before account pressure. Registration is a natural next step, not a wall.

### 4. Memory, not archive

History and cases should feel like *continuity* — "Nertura remembers your field" — not a cold log file.

### 5. Ownership without complexity

Farm and field surfaces should feel like *digital ownership* — my land, my crops — without ERP complexity.

### 6. Five-second comprehension

Every screen must answer within 5 seconds: *Where am I? What can I do? What happened?*

### 7. Conversation first (Doctor)

The AI Doctor is dialogue, not a form. Composer is primary; panels support, never compete.

### 8. Progress, not project management

Vaka Takibi feels like following a plant's story — not Jira for farms.

### 9. Transparency without pressure (Billing)

Pricing and limits are clear. No dark patterns. Upgrade is invitation, not trap.

### 10. Power without chaos (Admin)

Internal tools are dense when needed but never reckless. Approval-first for anything farmer-visible.

---

## Bad Practices

| Never | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Dashboard as homepage for farmers | Overwhelms; Doctor is home |
| Multiple competing CTAs above fold | Choice paralysis |
| Error screens with no next step | Breaks trust |
| Empty states that blame the user | Farmer-first copy required |
| AI answers that feel like a chatbot toy | Undermines agricultural credibility |
