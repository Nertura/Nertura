# Chapter 03 — Why Nertura Exists

## Purpose

Articulate the **problem Nertura solves** — for farmers, for agriculture, and for the team building it — so we never drift into building software farmers did not ask for.

---

## Principles

1. **Growers decide in the field, not in spreadsheets**
2. **Uncertainty is normal** — good AI acknowledges it
3. **Access beats expertise** — smallholders deserve the same intelligence as enterprises
4. **Speed of decision** matters more than volume of data
5. **Trust is earned one correct answer at a time**

---

## The Problem

### Farmer reality

| Pain | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Delayed diagnosis** | Extension agents unavailable; agronomists expensive; neighbors guess |
| **Information overload** | YouTube, WhatsApp groups, conflicting advice |
| **Generic AI failure** | ChatGPT does not know their crop, region, field history, or season |
| **Software complexity** | Farm ERPs built for accountants, not people in boots |
| **Language barriers** | Global tools default to English; local context ignored |
| **Input sales bias** | Many "advisors" are disguised marketplaces |

### Market gap

Existing solutions cluster at two broken extremes:

```
  TOO SIMPLE                    TOO COMPLEX
  ──────────                    ───────────
  Google search                   Farm ERP suites
  Generic ChatGPT                 Precision ag platforms
  Random photo apps               Consultant-only services
  
              ▲
              │
         NERTURA
    Simple entry + deep memory
    + trusted knowledge + neutral advice
```

---

## Our Answer

Nertura exists to be the **trusted intelligence layer** between the grower and the decision:

1. **Instant** — photo or question → actionable guidance in seconds
2. **Contextual** — crop, region, field, weather, history when available
3. **Honest** — confidence levels, evidence cards, "what to check next"
4. **Persistent** — field cases and memory across seasons
5. **Neutral** — no pesticide sales, no hidden affiliate agenda

---

## Decision Rationale

We chose **AI Doctor as entry** because:

- **Highest emotional urgency** — crop damage creates immediate need
- **Lowest setup friction** — no account required for first questions (guest Doctor)
- **Natural upsell to memory** — "Save this to your field" is logical, not salesy
- **Viral loop** — farmers share photos and answers in WhatsApp groups

We rejected **dashboard-first** because:

- Empty dashboards signal "work required before value"
- Smallholders do not self-identify as "SaaS users"
- Feature-rich home pages increase bounce rate on mobile

---

## Examples

### Real intake sentence (canonical)

> *"Osmaniye Toprakkale'de 10 dönüm buğday tarlam var, buğday sararıyor."*

Nertura must:

1. Extract location, area, crop, symptom
2. Confirm with user
3. Geocode → map
4. Draw boundary → save field
5. Open field case
6. Doctor continues with full context

This single sentence is **why Field Intelligence exists** — not because farmers asked for "polygon drawing tools."

### Bad alternative (what we don't build)

A 14-step onboarding form asking for tax ID, farm registration number, and irrigation system type before answering "why is my wheat yellow?"

---

## Best Practices

- Interview farmers with **their words**, not our module names
- Measure success by **time to first useful answer**, not feature adoption
- Design for **one-thumb mobile** in sunlight with gloves

## Bad Practices

- Assuming farmers want "digital transformation"
- Copying US-centric agtech UX for Turkish smallholders without adaptation
- Building features because competitors have them

---

## Future Considerations

- **Cooperative and ministry partnerships** — Nertura as intelligence backend
- **Offline-first modes** for low-connectivity regions
- **Expert escalation network** — human agronomists for low-confidence cases (premium)
